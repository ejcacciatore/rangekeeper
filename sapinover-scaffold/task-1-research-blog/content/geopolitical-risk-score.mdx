Every trader knows geopolitics moves markets. But how do you quantify "geopolitical risk" in a way that's actionable at 2:00 AM when Asian markets are open and Europe is about to wake up?

This is the problem we solved with our **GEO score**—a real-time 0-10 indicator that synthesizes multiple geopolitical intelligence feeds into a single, tradeable signal that updates every 15 minutes.

## The Problem with Traditional Geopolitical Analysis

Most geopolitical risk assessment is backwards-looking:

- **VIX only tells you market fear** (effect, not cause)
- **News headlines are reactive** (you're already late)
- **Academic indices update monthly** (useless for trading)
- **State department warnings are political** (not market-focused)

What traders need is a **forward-looking, continuously updated, market-specific** geopolitical risk measure that answers: "Should I reduce overnight exposure right now?"

## Our Methodology: Seven Data Streams

Our GEO score aggregates seven distinct intelligence streams:

### 1. Conflict Event Monitoring (30% weight)

We ingest real-time conflict event data from:
- **LiveUAmap:** Real-time conflict mapping (Ukraine, Middle East, Taiwan Strait, Korean Peninsula)
- **GDELT:** Global Database of Events, Language, and Tone (250+ sources, 100+ countries)
- **ACLED:** Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project

**What we track:**
- Kinetic military events (strikes, troop movements, naval deployments)
- Protest/civil unrest magnitude and geographic spread
- Terrorism incidents in market-relevant regions
- Border incidents and airspace violations

**Scoring logic:**
- Each event receives base severity score (1-10)
- Proximity to major financial centers increases weight
- Event clustering amplifies score (multiple events in 24h)
- Historical correlation with VIX spikes provides calibration

### 2. Sanctions & Trade Action (20% weight)

Real-time tracking of:
- New sanctions announcements (OFAC, EU, UN)
- Trade policy changes and tariff implementations
- Export control updates (semiconductors, defense tech)
- Strategic commodity restrictions

**Market impact calculation:**
- Affected GDP as % of global economy
- Supply chain disruption probability
- Historical precedent impact analysis

A major sanctions package can spike GEO by 2-3 points within 30 minutes of announcement.

### 3. Leadership & Political Stability (15% weight)

Monitoring:
- G20 leadership transitions and contested elections
- Central bank governor changes
- Military/intelligence leadership turnover
- Coalition government stability in key economies

**Signals we weight heavily:**
- Unexpected resignations (especially in Europe, Asia)
- Election results that deviate from final polls by >3%
- Central bank dissent in voting patterns
- Credit rating agency warnings

### 4. Strategic Asset Risk (15% weight)

Focus on critical market infrastructure and chokepoints:
- **Energy:** Strait of Hormuz, Suez Canal, Nord Stream status
- **Shipping:** South China Sea transit, Red Sea piracy, Panama Canal
- **Tech:** Undersea cable cuts, satellite interference, GPS jamming
- **Financial:** SWIFT restrictions, sovereign debt stress

Real-time vessel tracking (AIS data) detects abnormal naval deployments. We cross-reference with commodity futures for validation.

### 5. Cyber & Information Warfare (10% weight)

Tracking:
- State-sponsored cyber attacks on financial infrastructure
- Large-scale DDoS campaigns
- Disinformation operations targeting market narratives
- Exchange/clearing house security incidents

We maintain a severity matrix based on:
- Target criticality (clearing house > exchange > broker)
- Attribution confidence (state-sponsored vs criminal)
- Operational impact duration
- Geographic spread

### 6. Nuclear & WMD Posture (5% weight)

Low frequency but maximum impact:
- Strategic force readiness changes (DEFCON-equivalent indicators)
- Nuclear rhetoric from nuclear-armed states
- IAEA warnings and non-compliance reports
- Missile tests near critical sea lanes

This category has a **volatility amplifier**: Even small movements can spike GEO by 2+ points when base risk is already elevated.

### 7. Market-Specific Signals (5% weight)

Cross-validation with market data:
- Sovereign CDS spread acceleration
- Currency crisis indicators (reserves, peg stress)
- Flight-to-safety flows (UST, JPY, CHF, gold)
- Options skew in defense/aerospace sector

This prevents false positives—geopolitical events only matter if markets care.

## Scoring Normalization: 0-10 Scale

Raw input scores are normalized to our 0-10 scale:

- **0-2 (Green):** Baseline risk. Normal geopolitical noise.
- **3-4 (Yellow):** Elevated. Monitor specific regions/assets.
- **5-6 (Orange):** High. Reduce overnight leverage in affected sectors.
- **7-8 (Red):** Severe. Meaningful market impact probable within 24-48h.
- **9-10 (Critical):** Crisis. Major global market dislocation imminent/ongoing.

Historical calibration:
- COVID lockdown announcement (China, Jan 2020): 8.5
- Russia-Ukraine invasion (Feb 2022): 9.2
- Israel-Hamas war outbreak (Oct 2023): 7.8
- Silicon Valley Bank failure (Mar 2023): 6.5 (not primarily geopolitical but systematic)

## How Traders Use the GEO Score

### Risk Management
- **GEO > 7:** Many institutions have policies to reduce overnight exposure by 30-50%
- **GEO spike > 2 points in 1 hour:** Trigger for emergency risk review

- **GEO decline after spike:** Fade the fear trade (short VIX, long risk assets)

## Limitations & Future Development

### Current Limitations
1. **Geographic bias:** Better coverage of Western/Asian theaters than Africa/South America
2. **Language barrier:** Non-English primary sources have 30-60 min delay
3. **Attribution confidence:** Cyber events difficult to attribute quickly
4. **Market regime dependence:** Calibration assumes 2022-2026 market conditions

### Roadmap
- Add AI sentiment analysis of UN Security Council transcripts
- Integrate satellite imagery analysis for military positioning
- Machine learning recalibration based on actual market impact
- Regional GEO sub-scores (Asia, Europe, Middle East, Americas)
